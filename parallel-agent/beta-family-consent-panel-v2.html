What we collect during the beta

We only collect data needed to improve the product and make the beta program work. Here's exactly what, why, and for how long.

Data Why we collect it Retained
Child play sessions Improve learning engine and content recommendations Beta period + 90 days
Feedback you submit Fix bugs and improve features Permanently
App performance logs Debug crashes and slow load times 30 days
Beta journal entries Research insights and product direction Anonymised after beta
🚫 Not collected

We do NOT collect: location data, contacts, advertising IDs, device camera or microphone data, or any data not listed in the table above.

Your privacy rights

As a participant in the WonderQuest beta, you and your family have the following rights at all times.

🚪
Right to withdraw

You can leave the beta at any time. Simply email beta@wonderquest.com and we'll end your participation and begin data deletion within 30 days.

📋
Right to access your data

You can request a full export of all data we hold about your family at any time. We'll deliver it within 30 days in a portable format.

🗑️
Right to delete

You can request deletion of your family's data. We'll process it within 30 days. Anonymised research data may be retained per our privacy policy.

🔕
Right to opt out of research

You can opt out of having your family's data used for research at any time, while still participating in the beta program itself.

⚠ COPPA Notice — Children Under 13

As a parent or guardian, you are giving consent on behalf of your child. Your child cannot consent independently under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA, 16 CFR Part 312). You can withdraw consent at any time by emailing beta@wonderquest.com — we will stop collecting data and delete existing data within 30 days.

📚 FERPA Note — School-Linked Families

If your child's school is also participating in the beta, your child's educational records are additionally protected under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). You retain the right to inspect, review, and request corrections to those records.

🗃️ Consent Data Model
beta_consents ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY, user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id), consent_version TEXT NOT NULL, -- 'beta-v1.0' children_ids UUID[], agreed_at TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL, ip_hash TEXT, -- SHA-256 of IP, not raw IP user_agent_hash TEXT, -- SHA-256 of UA string attribution_opt_in BOOLEAN DEFAULT false, withdrawn_at TIMESTAMPTZ nullable, withdrawn_reason TEXT nullable )
⚖️ Legal Requirements
COPPA · 16 CFR Part 312
Children's Online Privacy Protection

Verifiable parental consent required before collecting personal information from children under 13. Parent must be identifiable; cannot rely on child self-reporting age.

FERPA
Educational Records Protection

If the child's school is a beta partner, student educational records are protected. Parents retain right to inspect, review, and amend records.

GDPR · Article 8
EU / UK Parental Consent Age

In the EU, parental consent required for children under 16. In the UK (post-Brexit UK GDPR), the age threshold is under 13. Consent must be verifiable.

Valid Consent Criteria
Consent must be…

Freely given — Specific — Informed — Unambiguous. Pre-ticked boxes or bundled consent does not satisfy GDPR standards. Each consent item must be granular.

🔐 Privacy by Design
🔒IP address stored as SHA-256 hash only — raw IP never persisted. Hash is non-reversible without the original value.
🔒User agent string stored as SHA-256 hash only — preserves fraud-audit trail without storing identifiable device strings.
🗑️Withdrawal sets withdrawn_at and triggers an automated data-deletion job that completes within 30 days.
🔄If consent terms change, re-consent is required. A new beta_consents row is created; the old row is not modified.
📝Feedback submitted by the parent is retained permanently but may be anonymised on request.
🎓Beta journal entries are anonymised after the beta period ends — research value preserved, identity removed.
✅ Consent Validation Rules (front-end)
All 4 required checkboxes must be in checked state before the "I Agree" button becomes enabled
Signature field must contain at least 2 characters (whitespace-trimmed) before "I Agree" is enabled
The optional attribution checkbox (ci-5) does not gate the submit button
The flow is NOT dismissible — no skip/close affordance exists; user must complete or navigate away entirely
On submission, consent_version is always set to 'beta-v1.0'; agreed_at is server-set UTC timestamp
attribution_opt_in reflects state of optional checkbox ci-5 at time of submission
