⚠ About readiness signals
Readiness signals are based on exploration patterns, not academic assessments. Moving to a new band is entirely your choice — Emma can stay in Explorer Band as long as she likes.
What Happens When Advancing
1
Parent or teacher sees readiness signal
WonderQuest sends a gentle notification when Emma's overall readiness score exceeds 80%. No action is required.
2
Parent or teacher chooses to advance (optional — not automatic)
Advancement is never automatic. The parent or teacher decides if and when Emma moves to the next band.
3
Child continues in new band — harder quests, wider themes
Emma's adventure continues in Trailblazer Band (P3) with new quests, skills, and subject areas unlocked for exploration.
4
If Emma finds the new band too challenging — she can always return
No lock-in. Emma (or her parent/teacher) can request to return to Explorer Band at any time. There is no penalty or negative signal.
5
Progress in previous band is always preserved
All of Emma's quest completions, XP, and stars from Explorer Band remain visible and saved regardless of which band she is currently in.

Specification — Item 260
Behaviour & Rules
Readiness is advisory
No automatic advancement. All band changes require an explicit action by a teacher or parent. The readiness scorecard is informational only.
Return path
Child can always request to return to a previous band. children.band_id is updateable. All previous progress is preserved — no data is deleted on band change.
Accuracy NOT used
Readiness is based solely on engagement depth: session frequency, quest completions, and return visits. Accuracy percentages are never surfaced in readiness scoring.
Child view
Children do NOT see the readiness scorecard. Children see only their adventure progress: quest completion, XP, and stars.
Teacher notification
When overall score > 80%, teacher and parent receive a gentle notification: "Emma may be ready to explore the next level — your choice!"
