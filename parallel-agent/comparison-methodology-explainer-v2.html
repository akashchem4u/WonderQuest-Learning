How We Compare — A Plain-Language Guide

For Emma's parents — understanding what WonderQuest comparisons actually mean

Section 1 — What is being compared?
WonderQuest compares how deeply children explore skills — not how many they get right.
We look at
  • How many skills your child has visited
  • How often they return to a skill
  • Whether they are progressing to harder challenges
  • Quest completions
We do NOT look at
  • How many questions were answered correctly
  • Speed of answers
  • Test scores
Section 2 — Who is Emma compared to?
👤 Age comparison
Other children who are 8 years old on WonderQuest.
🏫 Grade comparison
Other children in Grade 3 on WonderQuest.
Both use only WonderQuest data — not national tests or external databases.
Section 3 — What do the labels mean?
Exploring earlier
"Exploring this area more deeply than many children her age/grade"
Emma is exploring this area more deeply than many children her age or grade. Her curiosity is leading her into this territory.
Similar pace
"Exploration pattern is similar to many children her age/grade"
Emma's exploration pattern is similar to many children her age or grade. She's right in the heart of the WonderQuest community.
Just getting started
"Hasn't explored much in this area yet — that's completely fine!"
Emma hasn't explored much in this area yet — that's completely fine! Every child comes to new topics in their own time.
Section 4 — What this is NOT
Important — please read
  • This is not a test score.
  • This is not a prediction of academic performance.
  • This is not a diagnosis of any kind.
  • These comparisons exist only to help you spot patterns in Emma's interests.

Where the Comparison Data Comes From

A plain-language overview of the data sources used in WonderQuest comparisons

What data is used
  • Sessions completed on WonderQuest
  • Skills explored (topics visited)
  • Quests completed
  • Time of last visit (to detect active vs inactive periods)
What data is NOT used
  • Accuracy or correctness on individual questions
  • School grades or teacher assessments
  • External test scores (SATs, state assessments)
  • Data from other apps or services
  • Any personally identifying data from other children
Minimum cohort size
🔒
Privacy protection built in

Emma is only compared to groups of at least 50 other children — this protects everyone's privacy. If a group is too small, we show "Not enough data yet" rather than a potentially misleading comparison.
