Child Safety Rules

Prohibited Content

  • Scary, frightening, or horror-themed imagery
  • Violent or aggressive content of any kind
  • Gross, disgusting, or body-horror imagery
  • Realistic depictions of weapons or danger
  • Sexualized or adult content
  • Imagery that could cause distress in children aged 3–12

Content Filter Tags & Exclusion

  • Images may be tagged with filter labels: spiders, scary, dark, loud_colors, blood, insects, etc.
  • Tagged images are excluded from children who have those filters active in their profile
  • Filter tags do not prevent approval — they control visibility per child

WCAG AA Accessibility

  • Every image must have a non-empty alt_text before status can move to approved
  • Alt text must be reviewed and approved (accessibility_alt_approved = TRUE)
  • Decorative images must use alt="" explicitly — not omitted

Status Lifecycle

pending_review
  ├─→ approved   (child_safety_approved=TRUE, alt_text present,
  │               accessibility_alt_approved=TRUE)
  │     └─→ published  (content team publish action)
  └─→ rejected   (rejection_notes required)

Rejected images may be re-submitted for review after corrections. Published images cannot be un-published directly — open a content revision request.

Business Rules

1An image cannot be approved unless alt_text is present and accessibility_alt_approved is TRUE.
2An image cannot be approved unless child_safety_approved is explicitly set to TRUE by a reviewer.
3Rejection requires rejection_notes to be non-empty — rejections without explanation are not permitted.
4Filter-tagged images are automatically excluded from any child whose profile has the matching filter active, regardless of approval status.
5No child PII may be stored in image metadata fields (tags, alt_text, reviewed_by, etc.).
6Only users with role content_team or higher may approve, reject, or publish images. Read access is available to educator role.
