Home Practice Ideas
Easy activities for the week ahead
These ideas are based on what Maya is working on this week. Pick one or two that feel fun — even 5 minutes makes a difference! 🌱
🔤
Blend Hunt on a Walk
⏱ 5–10 min · Any time
1.
On your next walk, look for signs or labels
2.
Ask Maya: "Do you see a word that starts with two letters?"
3.
Say it together: "Bl-ue! Fl-ower!" Make it silly!
💡 Why this helps: Maya is working on blends (bl, cl, fl). Spotting them in real life builds recognition fast!
📖
Sight Word Flashcard Game
⏱ 5 min before bed
1.
Write 5 words on small cards: play, they, said, come, where
2.
Hold up each card and see how fast Maya can say it
3.
Celebrate every one she gets — clap, cheer, high five!
💡 Why this helps: Recognizing these words quickly makes reading more fluent and fun.
✍️
Draw and Label Something
⏱ 10 min · Weekend activity
1.
Ask Maya to draw her favorite animal or place
2.
Help her label parts of the drawing (ear, wing, lake...)
3.
Display it proudly! Maya loves to write — this plays to her strength
💡 Why this helps: Writing is Maya's strength — nurturing it builds confidence for all skills.

Parent Language Rules
NEVER show accuracy %, question counts correct/wrong, or raw mastery scores.
NEVER frame a metric as "missed" or "failed". Use "working on" or "building momentum".
NEVER show how many practice days were missed. Show days practiced, not days absent.
ALWAYS lead with wins (at least 1 win card before any support area).
Tier labels: "Support" (not "struggling"), "Progress" (not "average"), "Strength" (not "above grade").
Week comparison: Positive framing only. Flat or down week: "steady progress — every practice counts!"
