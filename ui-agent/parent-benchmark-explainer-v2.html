What do the labels mean?

WonderQuest uses three simple, growth-focused tiers to describe where your child is on each skill right now.

🌱
Opportunity

Just getting started

This skill is an exciting area ahead. Your child is beginning to explore it — every expert started here. This is where the biggest growth happens!

🌿
Growing

Building momentum

Your child is actively developing this skill. They're making real progress and are well on their way to mastery. Consistency now pays off big.

⭐
Strength

Confident & ready

Your child has built a strong foundation here. This skill is a genuine strength — something to celebrate and build on as they tackle new challenges.

A note from WonderQuest

No label is permanent. Skills shift as children practice, and an "Opportunity" today often becomes a "Strength" within weeks. We track your child's personal journey — not a race.

WonderQuest benchmarks are internal only — based on your child's own practice patterns and WonderQuest's curriculum design, not national tests or other children.

How we determine tiers

Our internal benchmark is built from how children typically progress through WonderQuest's curriculum — not from external standards.

Our 4-step process
1
Questions answered — We look at how many questions your child has attempted in each skill area over recent sessions.
2
Response patterns — We notice trends: are they getting more confident? Taking fewer attempts? Answering faster? All positive signs.
3
Consistency over time — A skill shown consistently across multiple sessions carries more weight than a single great day.
4
WonderQuest progression bands — We compare to our internal curriculum map: where do children in our system typically land on this skill at similar practice levels?

What we don't do

We never compare your child to other individual children, show ranked scores, or use standardized test benchmarks. Progress here is personal.

Frequently asked questions

Can my child move tiers quickly? ▼
Yes! Tiers update after every session. A focused practice week can move a skill from Opportunity to Growing very noticeably. We recalculate after each play session so the view stays current.
Is "Opportunity" something to worry about? ▼
Not at all. "Opportunity" simply means this is an area where practice will make the biggest difference right now. It's not a warning — it's a pointer to where WonderQuest will focus its magic next.
Does this connect to school grades? ▼
WonderQuest uses its own internal curriculum map, not school grade standards. While there is natural overlap in many skills, these tiers are specific to WonderQuest practice and don't directly map to report card grades.
How is this different from a test score? ▼
Test scores are a snapshot. WonderQuest tiers are a living picture — they reflect ongoing practice patterns over time, not a single performance moment. They also have no pass/fail framing. Every position is a valid place to be.

Talking about progress with your child

When they're in "Opportunity"

"WonderQuest found something new for you to explore! This is your adventure zone — want to try it together?"
"Every pro started right where you are. Let's see what happens when you practice this a few times!"

When they're "Growing"

"Look how much you've already figured out! You're getting better at this every time you play."
"WonderQuest can see you're making real progress here. How does it feel when you get one of these right?"

When they reach "Strength"

"You've built a real skill here — this is something you actually know now. That's amazing work!"
"You've mastered this one. Now WonderQuest is going to give you something even more exciting to try next!"

Keep it light

Children thrive when progress feels like an adventure, not an evaluation. Celebrate effort and curiosity first — the skills will follow.
