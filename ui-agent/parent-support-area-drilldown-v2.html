Practice Ideas
3 Ways to Practice Letter Sounds at Home
These activities are fun, quick, and directly support what Maya's working on right now.
1

Sound Safari Walk

5–10 min · Outside or around the house
Pick a letter of the day and take a "sound safari" — find as many things as you can that start with that sound. "B! Ball, bus, butterfly, bench!"
🌟 WonderQuest has a "Letter Sounds" quest in Dream World that makes this even more fun to review after!
Why it works: Connecting sounds to real objects is one of the fastest ways for young children to solidify phonics knowledge.
2

Vowel Song Sing-Along

5 min · Anytime
Make up a silly song to practice the vowel sounds: "A says /a/, E says /e/, I says /i/, O says /o/, U says /u/!" The sillier, the more it sticks.
🌟 After the song, open WonderQuest and find the vowel sounds practice — she'll recognize them!
3

Slow Stretch the Word

5 min · During meals or car rides
Say a simple word very slowly — like a rubber band being stretched: "ssss-uuuu-nnn" (sun). Ask Maya what sounds she hears. Start with 3-letter words like cat, sun, big, red.
🌟 This directly supports the blending skills coming next in WonderQuest.
Why it works: Segmenting words into individual sounds (phonemic awareness) is a critical pre-reading skill.
