Phonics Blending
K–1 · Reading Needs support
Phonics blending means putting letter sounds together to read a whole word — like hearing "c… a… t" and saying "cat". It's one of the most important early reading skills, and it takes practice to click.
Mia today
51%
Getting there — most children reach comfort at around 75%
✅
What Mia is doing well
  • Recognises most letter sounds on their own — strong foundation
  • Gets short two-letter blends right most of the time (like "at", "in")
  • Is trying — she replays the audio prompt often, which is exactly the right thing to do
🌱
What still needs a little practice
  • Three-letter blends are tricky — "str", "spl" type combinations
  • Sometimes rushes the answer before finishing the full sound
  • Silent-e words (like "cake", "bike") — a common sticking point at K–1
🏠
What to try at home
📖
Sound it out together
When reading together, pause on a new word and tap each letter sound slowly before saying the whole word. No pressure — keep it playful.
🎵
Sing blending songs
Songs and rhymes that break words into sounds work really well at this age. "Down by the Bay" is a favourite for rhyming practice.
⏱
5 minutes is plenty
Short, calm sessions beat long ones at this age. Even just one story a day where you slow down on new words helps a lot.
