Recommended Design Changes

Based on Round 3 observations — 3 primary recommendations for the design and engineering teams.

1

Fix audio coverage parity — all question types must have narration

Audio cutout on math questions was the single highest-severity friction observed. Children with lower literacy depend on audio narration to access question content independently. This is blocking for children aged 5–6.

Must-fix · P0
2

Reframe "Opportunity" tier label — add visual and verbal context for children

Three participants interpreted "Opportunity" negatively. Recommend adding a small character saying "This is your adventure zone — let's explore it!" when this tier label is shown to children. Parent-facing label can remain as-is per parent research.

Should-fix · P1
3

Increase answer card tap targets to minimum 44×44px, increase spacing

Current tap targets measure approximately 36×36px on 375px screens. Three children had repeated mis-taps that caused incorrect answers — potentially skewing their tier data. Recommend 44px minimum height with 8px vertical gap between cards.

Should-fix · P1
4

Consider hiding or softening the timer for ages 5–6

Younger participants showed visible anxiety when the timer reached low seconds. Recommend a behind-the-scenes timer (for adaptive pacing only) with no visible countdown for Pre-K and Grade 1, or replacing with a gentle progress indicator.

Consider · P2
