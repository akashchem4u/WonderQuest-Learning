Review pack publish criteria

Child-safety guardrails

Rule Applied behavior
No fear spike languageMessages stay curious and steady. "Look closely," "Let's help," and "Try another path" replace warnings or panic.
No predator-prey loopsScenes cannot imply hunting, trapping, or chase success. Running animations are decorative only and never framed as pursuit.
No unsafe realismWild animals are not framed as pets or touch targets. All interaction stays symbolic: tracks, badges, journals, stars, and habitat cards.

Review board checklist

Check Owner
Species accuracy approvedCommon name, habitat, and sound labels match approved glossary. Content ops
Error copy approvedNo blame language in any animal-specific recovery state. Child safety

Allowed

Helper-first creature design

Guides wave, point, or celebrate. They do not lunge, roar, or surprise-enter the frame.

Blocked

Competitive capture framing

Any line that tells the child to catch, trap, or outrun an animal fails child-safety review.

Allowed

Habitat respect messaging

Parent and teacher specs can reference "homes," "care," and "keeping distance" without sounding instructional or moralizing.

Blocked

Scare-based intensity

No flash-cut silhouettes, loud stingers, or red alert styling in any child-facing state.

Rotation behavior

This pack can be suggested when a child has at least one matching interest tag and no explicit animal dislike. Rotation stays cosmetic only and never changes content difficulty, band, or scoring thresholds.

Cooldown Do not repeat the same animal sub-pack inside a 14-day window unless the parent pins it.
Suppression If a child marks ocean sounds as overwhelming, suppress the Ocean Rescue line while keeping forest and safari available.

Launch criteria

All three sub-packs must have approved child copy, reviewed ambient audio, and a completed accessibility pass before the pack can leave owner review.

Family promise

The animal pack is optional, reversible, and transparent. Parent controls can disable it instantly, and the child never sees language implying that a theme choice affects progress, stars, or placement.
