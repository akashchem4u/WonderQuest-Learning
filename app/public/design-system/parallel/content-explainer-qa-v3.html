Student-Facing Explainer (as rendered) P1 K–1 · Science · Plants
✨ Great try! Here's a hint to help you…
Plants are like little food factories! They use sunlight to make their own food. When sunlight hits the green leaves, the plant uses it to turn air and water into energy — a process called photosynthesis. Without sunlight, a plant can't make food and will stop growing.
🌱 Try this: Put a plant in a dark cupboard for a week and watch what happens. Now put it back in the sun. What do you notice?
💜 Fun fact: Some plants, like mushrooms, don't need sunlight at all! They get energy from things in the soil instead.
