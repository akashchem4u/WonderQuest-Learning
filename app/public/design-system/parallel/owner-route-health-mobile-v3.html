Component: owner-route-health-mobile-v3.html
Mobile route health monitoring. Same 10 routes as desktop — card layout. P0 banner non-dismissible. Routes tab icon turns red on incident.

Layout Differences from Desktop

Desktop: full table with latency small multiples + sidebar. Mobile: route cards with dot/name/p95/uptime. Latency sparklines omitted on mobile. Sidebar meta collapsed into bottom incident log card. Expand on tap shows inline detail for down/degraded routes.

P0 Non-Dismissible Banner

The incident banner at top is rendered above the status strip and cannot be dismissed by the user. It clears automatically when the incident is resolved (route returns to healthy + PagerDuty closed). Also appears on owner home mobile screen.

Routes Tab Indicator

During active P0: Routes tab icon in bottom nav turns red (#f85149). During P1: amber (#f59e0b). This matches the convention used on owner home mobile where the Routes tile shows degraded state.

Refresh Indicator

Normal: "Re-check" button in header. During incident: header shows "10s ●" with blinking green dot (CSS animation) indicating auto-refresh every 10s. This replaces the "Re-check" text to indicate the system is already polling aggressively.
